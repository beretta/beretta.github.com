Dr. Stefano Beretta is a highly accomplished senior bioinformatician at the [San Raffaele Telethon Institute for Gene Therapy (TIGET)](https://www.hsr.it/ricerca/struttura/sr-tiget/) in Milan, Italy, with expertise in computer science, computational biology, gene therapy, and cell therapy. As a member of TIGET's Bioinformatics Core, he plays a key role in supporting high-impact research across multiple units and projects. Dr. Beretta excels at developing and optimizing pipelines for large-scale genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, and metabolomic analyses, with a focus on identifying genetic biomarkers and refining therapeutic targets. He is an expert in different technologies, ranging from bulk to single-cell, including spatial techniques, and targeted sequencing approaches. He is highly skilled in Bash, Python, and R programming, as well as in leveraging bioinformatics tools and cloud platforms for high-throughput data analysis.

{: .text-justify}
Dr. Beretta's research activity focuses on the development and analysis of efficient algorithms, particularly within the domain of bioinformatics. His core interests lie in the design and implementation of combinatorial algorithms tailored to address complex biological challenges. He is deeply engaged in the study and practical application of algorithms, especially for biological sequence analysis. He also maintains a strong interest in computational complexity theory, and is actively exploring the application of machine learning and artificial intelligence techniques to enhance his research, aiming to develop hybrid algorithms and AI-driven tools. He is a proven leader and collaborator, adept at communicating complex scientific insights.

A detailed overview of his scientific activities is provided in his [Curriculum Vitae (English).](/assets/files/CV-Beretta-Stefano.pdf)
